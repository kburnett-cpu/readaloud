# Illustration Prompt Guide: The Lighthouse Mystery

## Master Style

MASTER STYLE: Vibrant, warm children's book illustration in soft digital painting style. Clean outlines with rich saturated colors. Main characters: Marisol, a Dominican girl about ten years old with warm brown skin, thick black hair in two puffs, wearing a bright coral tank top and denim shorts; Diego, her younger brother about seven years old with warm brown skin, short curly black hair, wearing a yellow striped shirt and khaki shorts; Abuelo Rafael, a Dominican grandfather in his seventies with deep brown skin, close-cropped white hair, a white guayabera shirt, and kind weathered eyes. Setting is a small tropical Caribbean island in the Dominican Republic with turquoise water, lush green palm trees, colorful bougainvillea, and warm golden sunlight. 16:9 landscape format.

## Page Prompts

### Page 1 — `page-01.webp`
> Marisol and her brother Diego sailed to their grandfather's small island for the whole summer.

A wide ocean view under a bright blue Caribbean sky as a small wooden sailboat glides across sparkling turquoise water toward a lush green island in the distance. Marisol, a Dominican girl about ten with warm brown skin and thick black hair in two puffs wearing a coral tank top and denim shorts, leans eagerly over the bow, pointing at the island ahead. Diego, her younger brother about seven with warm brown skin and short curly black hair wearing a yellow striped shirt, sits beside her gripping the railing with wide excited eyes. Golden sunlight dances on the water and tropical birds circle the distant treetops. The mood is joyful, adventurous, and full of summer promise. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 2 — `page-02.webp`
> Abuelo's house was bright yellow with a red tin roof, sitting right beside the turquoise sea.

A cheerful bright yellow house with a red tin roof sits nestled among lush tropical greenery right at the edge of shimmering turquoise water. Tall swaying palm trees and vivid pink bougainvillea frame the house, and a small wooden dock stretches out over the sea. Marisol and Diego stand in the sandy yard with wide smiles, their small bags dropped at their feet, gazing up at the colorful home. Warm golden afternoon light bathes the entire scene in a welcoming glow. The mood is cozy, bright, and full of summer arrival joy. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 3 — `page-03.webp`
> On their very first walk, Marisol spotted a tall, dark lighthouse at the top of the rocky hill.

Marisol and Diego walk along a rocky sun-dappled path winding up a green hillside, tropical plants and wildflowers on either side. At the top of the hill silhouetted against a dramatic golden-orange sky stands a tall dark lighthouse, its stone tower weathered and slightly foreboding. Marisol stops mid-step, one hand shading her eyes as she stares up at the lighthouse with an expression of intense curious wonder. Diego stands just behind her, mouth slightly open in awe. Long late-afternoon shadows stretch across the rocky ground. The mood is mysterious and intriguing with a tingle of adventure. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 4 — `page-04.webp`
> Abuelo's eyes grew quiet when they asked about it, and he said only, "That old place is forgotten."

Abuelo Rafael, a Dominican grandfather in his seventies with deep brown skin, close-cropped white hair, and a white guayabera shirt, stands on the sunny porch of the yellow house as Marisol and Diego look up at him with eager curious faces. Abuelo's warm kind eyes have gone quiet and distant, his expression careful and a little sad, his hands still on the porch railing. Marisol gestures back toward the hilltop and Diego leans forward expectantly. A soft breeze stirs the bougainvillea and a shadow passes briefly over Abuelo's face. The mood is tender but gently mysterious, with unspoken emotion in the air. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 5 — `page-05.webp`
> But Marisol and Diego were not the kind of children who could leave a mystery alone.

Marisol and Diego stand together on a sunlit hillside path facing the dark distant lighthouse at the top of the rocky hill, both with their arms crossed and determined grins on their faces. Marisol tilts her chin up boldly and Diego mirrors her confident stance with fists on his hips. Their eyes sparkle with resolve and playful stubbornness. Bright tropical flowers and green ferns surround the path and butterflies drift past them. The mood is plucky, confident, and energetically adventurous. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 6 — `page-06.webp`
> The next morning they pushed open the heavy lighthouse door and found dusty stairs spiraling up into the dark.

Marisol and Diego push together against a massive heavy wooden lighthouse door with rusted iron hinges, straining with effort as the door creaks slowly open. A shaft of warm morning light spills through the gap into a dim dusty interior revealing a spiraling stone staircase disappearing upward into shadows. Marisol pushes hard with both hands and Diego leans his whole small body into the door. Dust motes swirl in the beam of light and old cobwebs drift in the corners. The mood is tense with excitement, courageous, and thrillingly mysterious. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 7 — `page-07.webp`
> At the very top, tucked beneath a broken lantern, Diego found an old logbook with water-stained pages.

Diego kneels on the stone floor at the very top of the lighthouse beside a large broken iron lantern housing covered in dust and dried sea salt. He carefully lifts a thick old logbook with warped water-stained brown covers from the shadows beneath the broken lantern, his face illuminated by pale light coming through the cracked glass windows. Marisol crouches beside him, one hand over her mouth in astonished delight. Pale misty ocean light filters through the grime-covered windows and the vast Caribbean sea is visible in the background far below. The mood is wondrous, hushed, and filled with the thrill of discovery. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 8 — `page-08.webp`
> The pages were filled with strange symbols and numbers that neither of them could read right away.

Marisol and Diego sit close together on the dusty lighthouse floor with the open old logbook spread across their laps. The yellowed water-stained pages are covered in rows of strange symbols, dots, dashes, and handwritten numbers in faded brown ink. Both children lean in close, brows furrowed in concentration, fingers tracing the mysterious markings. Marisol tilts her head to one side studying the symbols and Diego points at one cluster of marks with a puzzled squint. Soft filtered light from the old windows casts a gentle glow over the pages. The mood is puzzled, intrigued, and quietly intense. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 9 — `page-09.webp`
> Marisol said, "It is a code — we can break it together, one page at a time."

Marisol holds the open logbook up confidently in both hands, a bright determined smile on her face, as she turns to Diego with sparkling eyes. Diego looks up at her with an eager hopeful grin, ready for the challenge. They stand together in the golden morning light outside the lighthouse, tropical greenery all around them. Marisol's expression radiates certainty and warm leadership, her coral top bright in the sunshine. The mood is energized, optimistic, and full of cooperative sibling spirit. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 10 — `page-10.webp`
> Day after day they worked at Abuelo's kitchen table, matching symbols to letters until words appeared.

Marisol and Diego sit together at a large wooden kitchen table inside Abuelo's cozy colorful home, the logbook open beside sheets of paper covered in their handwritten notes and letter charts. Colored pencils, a ruler, and a hand-drawn symbol key are spread across the table. Marisol traces a line carefully on her notes and Diego erases and rewrites a letter, both fully absorbed. Warm afternoon light streams through an open window hung with bright curtains and a vase of tropical flowers sits on the counter behind them. The mood is diligent, warm, and happily industrious across many days. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 11 — `page-11.webp`
> The logbook told of a ship called La Esperanza that sank in a fierce storm fifty years ago.

A richly detailed close-up of an open logbook page showing Marisol and Diego's decoded text emerging beside the original symbols, with the words 'La Esperanza' written in large decoded letters circled in pencil. A small hand-drawn sketch of a ship in a fierce storm appears in the margin of the page. Marisol's finger points to the ship's name with wide astonished eyes and Diego's mouth forms a perfect O of surprise. The page glows warmly under the kitchen light and the mood is electrifying with the rush of a major discovery. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 12 — `page-12.webp`
> Then one entry made Marisol's heart stop — it said the keeper who let the light go dark was named Rafael Matos.

A dramatic close-up of the open logbook page in Marisol's trembling hands, one particular line of decoded text highlighted by her shaking fingertip that reads 'keeper Rafael Matos let the light go dark.' Marisol's face is frozen in shock, eyes wide and breath caught, her expression shifting from excitement to stunned disbelief. Diego leans over her shoulder, his small face creased with worry as he reads the name. Warm kitchen light glows around them but the mood has shifted to heavy, breathless, and full of dawning dread. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 13 — `page-13.webp`
> Rafael Matos was Abuelo's name, and when they showed him the page, tears ran slowly down his brown cheeks.

Abuelo Rafael sits in his wooden rocking chair on the porch, and Marisol gently holds the open logbook out to him with both hands, showing him the decoded page. The moment he reads it, slow silent tears begin to trail down his deep brown weathered cheeks, his hands gripping the chair arms. Diego stands just behind Marisol, his face full of confused compassion, reaching out a small hand toward Abuelo. The warm Caribbean late-afternoon light softens the scene in golden amber. The mood is deeply emotional, sorrowful, and tender with old grief finally surfacing. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 14 — `page-14.webp`
> He sat down, folded his hands, and said, "I was only a boy, I was scared, and I have carried this secret alone for so long."

Abuelo Rafael sits forward in his rocking chair, his large weathered hands folded tightly in his lap, head slightly bowed as he speaks. His deep brown face carries fifty years of quiet weight but also a fragile relief at finally speaking. Marisol and Diego sit on the porch steps below him, both turned toward him with open attentive faces, listening with full hearts. Soft evening light turns the sky behind them pink and gold and the sea shimmers gently in the distance. The mood is confessional, tender, deeply human, and full of long-withheld emotion. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 15 — `page-15.webp`
> He told them everything — the storm, the broken lamp, the guilt that had lived in his chest like a stone.

Abuelo sits in his rocking chair with his eyes closed, hands open in his lap, as he tells his story. Around him the illustration shows a soft dreamlike memory inset — a young brown-skinned teenage boy standing alone at the base of a dark lighthouse during a raging storm, a broken oil lamp in his hands, terror on his young face as lightning illuminates angry black waves. Marisol and Diego listen in the present, their faces full of empathy. The two layers of time — present and memory — are blended in warm and stormy tones. The mood is haunting, compassionate, and full of the weight of old guilt. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 16 — `page-16.webp`
> Marisol took Abuelo's hand and said, "You have carried this long enough — now we carry it with you."

Marisol reaches across and gently takes Abuelo Rafael's large rough hand in both of her small brown ones, looking up at his face with absolute steadfast love and courage. Abuelo looks down at her with glistening eyes, surprised and moved by her tenderness. Diego reaches over and places his small hand on top of theirs, completing the stack of three hands together. The golden evening light wraps around all three of them warmly. The mood is profoundly loving, courageous, and quietly triumphant — the weight of the secret beginning to lift. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 17 — `page-17.webp`
> Together the three of them walked to the lighthouse and left flowers at the door for the sailors of La Esperanza.

Marisol, Diego, and Abuelo Rafael walk together up the rocky hillside path toward the tall lighthouse in the soft golden light of early morning. Each of them carries a handful of bright tropical flowers — red hibiscus, yellow alamanda, and purple bougainvillea. Abuelo walks between the two children, a hand resting gently on each of their shoulders. The lighthouse stands above them against a pastel sky and the turquoise sea glimmers below. The mood is solemn, healing, and quietly beautiful — a family honoring the past together. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 18 — `page-18.webp`
> Abuelo stood straighter after that, and his laugh, warm as the Caribbean sun, came back little by little.

Abuelo Rafael stands tall and straight outside the bright yellow house in the morning sunlight, his white guayabera crisp and clean, his posture open and unburdened for the first time. He throws his head back in a warm deep laugh as Marisol and Diego chase a pelican around the yard, arms flailing and giggling wildly. Colorful tropical birds perch in the palm trees and bougainvillea blazes pink behind him. His whole face is alive with genuine joy that reaches his eyes. The mood is warm, light, free, and gloriously happy — a man returned to himself. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 19 — `page-19.webp`
> Marisol wrote the whole true story in a new notebook so the past would never be forgotten again.

Marisol sits at the kitchen table by the open window with a brand-new notebook open in front of her, writing carefully and deliberately with a pencil. Beside her the old logbook lies closed, its story finished. She looks down at her writing with a focused peaceful expression, one hand steadying the page. Through the window the lighthouse is visible on the hill against a bright blue sky and the sea sparkles in the distance. The mood is purposeful, calm, and full of the quiet power of storytelling and remembrance. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

The turquoise sea stretches wide and radiant behind them. A single page from the new notebook flutters open in the breeze in the corner of the scene.
