# Illustration Prompt Guide: The Day the River Changed

## Master Style

MASTER STYLE: Vibrant, warm children's book illustration in soft digital painting style. Clean outlines with rich saturated colors. Main character is Valentina, a curious and determined Dominican girl, approximately 9 years old, with warm brown skin, thick dark curly hair pulled into two puffs with yellow hair ties, wearing a bright turquoise short-sleeved shirt with small white flowers and denim shorts with brown sandals. Setting is a lively Caribbean Dominican town with brightly painted homes in coral, yellow, lime green, and sky blue, lush tropical plants including banana trees, hibiscus, and palm fronds, and a wide river curving through the landscape under warm golden sunlight. Supporting characters include warm brown-skinned Dominican neighbors, an elderly man in a straw hat, a kind female teacher in a floral blouse, and enthusiastic diverse brown-skinned Dominican schoolchildren in school uniforms of white shirts and navy bottoms. Art style references the warmth of Yuyi Morales and Leo Espinosa — expressive faces, rich texture, joyful energy. 16:9 landscape format.

## Page Prompts

### Page 1 — `page-01.webp`
> Valentina loved the wide brown river that curved behind her colorful town.

Wide sweeping aerial-style view of a vibrant Dominican town with colorful coral, yellow, and lime-green painted homes nestled among lush tropical palms and banana trees, a wide brown river curving gracefully behind the town under a warm golden sky. In the foreground, Valentina — a 9-year-old Dominican girl with warm brown skin, thick dark curly hair in two puffs with yellow ties, wearing a turquoise flower-print shirt and denim shorts — stands at the riverbank smiling broadly with her arms open wide, taking in the view. Bright sunlight sparkles on the water's surface, and colorful birds perch in the trees above. The mood is joyful, warm, and full of wonder. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 2 — `page-02.webp`
> Every morning, she watched silver fish dart through the clear, cool water.

Close-up view of the river's surface glittering with morning light, with dozens of small silver fish darting playfully in schools through clear, cool, blue-green water, their scales catching the sun in tiny flashes. Valentina kneels at the mossy riverbank, leaning forward with delight, her warm brown face lit up with a wide smile, her dark curly puffs framed by golden morning light. Tropical plants — broad green leaves and pink hibiscus — frame the edges of the scene. The mood is magical, peaceful, and full of life. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 3 — `page-03.webp`
> One day, the water looked dark and smelled strange, and no fish came.

The same riverbank shown in earlier pages, but now the water is dark brownish-green and murky, with an oily sheen on the surface — no fish are visible anywhere. Valentina stands at the water's edge, her brow furrowed and her nose wrinkled, looking down at the strange dark water with worry and confusion on her warm brown face. She holds her hand up near her nose as if detecting a bad smell, and the surrounding tropical plants look slightly wilted. The sky above has a slightly hazy, overcast quality compared to earlier bright scenes. The mood is alarmed, puzzling, and somber. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 4 — `page-04.webp`
> Valentina knelt at the bank and scooped some water into a clean glass jar.

Valentina crouches carefully at the muddy riverbank, her knees bent and brown sandals near the water's edge, holding a clean clear glass jar with both hands as she scoops up a sample of the dark murky river water. Her expression is focused and serious — eyes narrowed with concentration, lips pressed together — like a young scientist on a mission. The jar glows slightly as she holds it up, the dark water contrasting against the bright tropical greenery behind her. Sunlight filters through banana leaves overhead, casting dappled light on the scene. The mood is determined, methodical, and curious. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 5 — `page-05.webp`
> She walked along the river asking neighbors, "When did the water change?"

Valentina walks along a dirt path beside the river, holding her glass jar of dark water in one hand and a small yellow notebook in the other, approaching a group of three Dominican neighbors — an older woman hanging laundry, a middle-aged man mending a fishing net, and a young mother with a toddler on her hip — all pausing to listen to her. Valentina's expression is earnest and inquisitive, her mouth open mid-question, looking up at the neighbors with wide curious eyes. Colorful painted homes in coral and yellow are visible in the background, and large tropical leaves frame the path. The mood is investigative, community-spirited, and warm. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 6 — `page-06.webp`
> Old Don Ramón said, "It started three weeks ago, after the new factory opened upstream."

An old Dominican man, Don Ramón, sits on a wooden stool outside his sky-blue painted home, wearing a weathered straw hat, a white guayabera shirt, and worn trousers, gesturing upstream with one wrinkled hand while speaking seriously. Valentina stands before him, clutching her yellow notebook and pencil, writing down what he says with intense focus, her dark curly puffs framing her concentrated expression. In the far background, barely visible above the treetops, the angular shape of a factory building with a smokestack can be seen upstream. Tropical flowers and a mango tree frame the doorway of Don Ramón's home. The mood is revelatory, serious, and purposeful. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 7 — `page-07.webp`
> Valentina carried her water sample to the school library and opened every book she could find.

The interior of a bright, cheerful school library filled with colorful bookshelves, potted tropical plants on the windowsills, and warm afternoon light streaming through louvered windows. Valentina sits at a wooden reading table, her glass jar of dark water sitting beside a stack of opened books and her yellow notebook, surrounded by pages of illustrations of rivers and ecosystems. Her expression is deeply absorbed — leaning forward on her elbows, eyes wide and scanning a large open book with illustrated diagrams of clean and polluted water. The scene feels cozy, intellectual, and full of discovery. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 8 — `page-08.webp`
> She read that dark, oily water means something harmful has been dumped inside it.

Close view of Valentina at the library table, now sitting upright with an expression of sudden realization — eyebrows raised, mouth forming a small 'O' of understanding — as she points to an open book showing illustrations of dark oily water and warning symbols beside a factory icon. Her yellow notebook is open beside her with handwritten notes and small sketched diagrams. The glass jar of murky river water sits on the table catching the light. Colorful book spines line the shelves in the warm background. The mood is illuminating, exciting, and empowering. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 9 — `page-09.webp`
> Valentina showed her jar and her notes to her teacher, Señora Cruz, who listened carefully.

Inside a bright classroom with large windows letting in tropical afternoon light, Valentina stands at the front beside her teacher, Señora Cruz — a warm Dominican woman in her 40s with brown skin, dark hair in a neat bun, wearing a yellow floral blouse and dark slacks — who sits on the edge of her desk and listens with a thoughtful, caring expression, chin resting in her hand. Valentina holds up both the glass jar of dark water and her open yellow notebook filled with handwritten notes and sketched diagrams, explaining with an earnest and hopeful expression. A colorful classroom map of the Dominican Republic is visible on the wall. The mood is collaborative, supportive, and hopeful. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 10 — `page-10.webp`
> Together, the whole class made bright posters that said, "Save Our River, Save Our Fish!"

A cheerful, bustling classroom scene showing a dozen enthusiastic Dominican schoolchildren in white shirts and navy bottoms working together at tables and on the floor, painting and decorating large bright poster boards with bold green, blue, and yellow letters reading 'SAVE OUR RIVER, SAVE OUR FISH!' and drawings of fish, rivers, and trees. Valentina is in the center of the group, laughing and helping a classmate paint a large blue fish with her turquoise shirt spotted with green paint. Señora Cruz smiles proudly in the background. Finished posters are propped against the colorful classroom walls. The mood is joyful, energetic, and united. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 11 — `page-11.webp`
> Every student wrote a letter to the mayor asking him to stop the factory from dumping waste.

A classroom scene showing every student seated at their desk, bent over paper with pencils and pens in hand, writing their letters with focused and serious expressions — some chewing pencil ends thoughtfully, others pressing down with determination. Valentina sits at the front desk, her letter already half-filled with neat handwriting, pausing to re-read what she has written with a proud, purposeful expression. Stacks of finished envelopes are beginning to pile on Señora Cruz's desk at the side. Posters from the previous scene are visible on the walls. The mood is civic, determined, and meaningful. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 12 — `page-12.webp`
> At the town meeting, Valentina stood up tall and read her letter in a clear, strong voice.

A wide view of a crowded Dominican town meeting hall with wooden chairs filled with brown-skinned adult community members of all ages, colorful hand-painted posters propped along the walls, and warm lantern-style lights overhead. At a simple podium at the front, Valentina stands tall and straight, chin lifted, one hand holding her letter open, looking out at the audience with a clear, courageous expression — bright eyes steady and voice strong. Her classmates sit in a row behind her, watching proudly. A heavyset Dominican mayor in a guayabera shirt sits at a table to the side, leaning forward and listening attentively. The mood is courageous, powerful, and inspiring. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 13 — `page-13.webp`
> The mayor promised to inspect the factory, and soon the river ran clean and the fish came back.

A glorious final scene at the riverbank bathed in bright golden sunlight: the river now runs clear and sparkling blue-green, and dozens of silver fish leap and dart through the clean water. Valentina stands barefoot at the water's edge, arms raised triumphantly above her head and face split into a huge beaming smile, her dark curly puffs silhouetted against the bright sky. Her classmates stand beside her cheering and laughing, some splashing in the shallows. In the background, colorful Dominican homes peek through lush tropical palms and banana trees. A small group of smiling neighbors including Don Ramón waves from the bank. The mood is triumphant, joyful, and full of hope. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.
