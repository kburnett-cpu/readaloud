# Illustration Prompt Guide: The Last Old Tree

## Master Style

MASTER STYLE: Vibrant, warm children's book illustration in soft digital painting style. Clean outlines with rich saturated colors. Main characters are Valentina, a spirited 8-year-old Dominican girl with warm brown skin, thick black curly hair in two puffs, wearing a bright yellow dress with white trim, and Miguel, a cheerful 8-year-old Dominican boy with warm brown skin, short black hair, wearing a red striped shirt and khaki shorts. Supporting cast includes warm brown-skinned Dominican abuelas in floral housedresses and abuelos in guayaberas, and Señora Rosa, a round-faced Dominican woman in her 40s with silver-streaked hair in a bun wearing a teal blouse. Setting is Calle Verde, a lively Caribbean Dominican neighborhood with colorful painted homes in turquoise, coral, yellow, and pink, lush tropical plants including hibiscus, bougainvillea, and palm fronds, warm golden sunlight, and a massive ancient mahogany tree with sprawling thick branches and dense green canopy towering over every rooftop. 16:9 landscape format.

## Page Prompts

### Page 1 — `page-01.webp`
> On Calle Verde, one giant old tree stood above every rooftop in the neighborhood.

A sweeping wide-angle view of a vibrant Dominican neighborhood street called Calle Verde at golden hour, showing a row of colorful painted homes in coral, turquoise, yellow, and pink with wrought-iron balconies draped in bougainvillea. At the center of the scene, an enormous ancient mahogany tree with a massive gnarled trunk and a wide spreading canopy of deep green leaves towers dramatically above every rooftop on the block, its branches stretching across the entire sky. Warm late-afternoon sunlight filters through the leaves, casting dappled patterns across the cobblestone street below. The mood is magical, peaceful, and awe-inspiring, emphasizing how grand and irreplaceable the old tree truly is. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 2 — `page-02.webp`
> Children climbed its thick branches after school every single day.

Valentina, an 8-year-old Dominican girl with warm brown skin and thick black curly hair in two puffs wearing a bright yellow dress with white trim, laughs joyfully as she climbs high up into the thick gnarled branches of the giant mahogany tree. Below and around her, four or five other brown-skinned Dominican children of similar age scramble up the lower branches, hanging and laughing, school backpacks dropped at the base of the trunk. The late afternoon sun shines warm and golden through the dense canopy of leaves, and the colorful Caribbean homes of Calle Verde are visible in the background. The mood is joyful, energetic, and full of childhood wonder. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 3 — `page-03.webp`
> Abuelas and abuelos rested in its cool, wide shade every afternoon.

Three elderly Dominican abuelas in bright floral housedresses and two abuelos in crisp white and pale blue guayabera shirts sit together in weathered wooden chairs and a painted bench nestled in the deep blue-green shade beneath the massive mahogany tree. Their faces are relaxed and content as they chat and fan themselves with folded paper fans, cups of coffee resting on a small table. Beams of warm sunlight filter gently through the canopy above, and tropical flowers bloom around the tree's base. The mood is tranquil, nostalgic, and deeply communal. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 4 — `page-04.webp`
> Every spring, bright yellow birds built soft nests high up in its leaves.

A close-up and slightly elevated view of the upper branches of the giant mahogany tree in springtime, where two small bright yellow birds with orange-tipped wings tend to a beautifully woven soft nest tucked snugly into a fork between two thick branches. The nest holds three tiny pale blue eggs, and the surrounding leaves are a lush, vivid green with soft sunlight glowing through them. Below in the blurred background, the colorful rooftops of the Dominican neighborhood are visible. The mood is delicate, hopeful, and quietly miraculous. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 5 — `page-05.webp`
> One Monday morning, a big orange sign appeared nailed to the trunk.

A bright Monday morning scene on Calle Verde showing the base of the giant mahogany tree with a large, jarring orange construction sign nailed directly onto its ancient bark, the metal staples biting into the rough textured trunk. The orange sign is bold and official-looking against the warm brown of the tree. The surrounding neighborhood is cheerful and colorful as usual, with hibiscus blooming nearby and a turquoise house behind, which makes the intrusive orange sign feel even more alarming and out of place. A few fallen leaves rest at the base of the tree as if in quiet mourning. The mood is ominous, unsettling, and full of foreboding contrast. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 6 — `page-06.webp`
> The sign said the old tree would be cut down to make a parking lot.

A close-up on the orange sign nailed to the ancient mahogany trunk, clearly showing large bold text that reads 'THIS TREE WILL BE REMOVED — NEW PARKING LOT COMING SOON' stamped with an official government seal. Around the sign, the deeply furrowed bark of the old tree shows decades of growth and character, with tiny moss patches and carved initials barely visible. In the blurred background, the colorful Dominican street and a small construction company truck can be seen parked at the curb. The mood is shocking, unjust, and deeply sad. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 7 — `page-07.webp`
> Valentina, Miguel, and their classmates read the sign and felt their hearts drop.

Valentina, in her bright yellow dress with white trim and two black curly puffs, stands side by side with Miguel, in his red striped shirt and khaki shorts, along with four other brown-skinned Dominican classmates in school uniforms, all gathered in a cluster at the base of the mahogany tree staring up at the orange sign. Their faces show a mix of shock, sadness, and dawning determination — eyebrows furrowed, mouths slightly open, eyes wide. One child covers her mouth with her hand, another crosses his arms tightly. The afternoon light has shifted to a slightly cooler tone, emphasizing the emotional weight of the moment. The mood is heartbroken, disbelieving, and quietly resolute. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 8 — `page-08.webp`
> Their teacher, Señora Rosa, helped them search old books to learn about the tree.

Inside a warm, cheerful Dominican school classroom decorated with paper flowers and student artwork on the walls, Señora Rosa — a round-faced Dominican woman in her 40s with silver-streaked hair in a bun wearing a teal blouse — sits at a table surrounded by Valentina, Miguel, and six classmates. The table is covered with large open old books, aged maps, and yellowed photographs. Señora Rosa points to a faded photo in one of the books while the children lean in with wide curious eyes, their expressions shifting from sadness to fascination. Warm afternoon light streams through louvered classroom windows. The mood is curious, engaged, and filled with growing purpose. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 9 — `page-09.webp`
> They discovered the tree was planted eighty years ago by families who once lived right there.

Valentina and Miguel, their faces illuminated by wonder, lean over a large open old book on the classroom table that shows a faded sepia photograph of the same street — Calle Verde — from eighty years ago, with a tiny young sapling tree being planted by a group of smiling Dominican families dressed in 1940s clothing. A handwritten caption below the photo is partially visible. Around the book, other historical documents and a hand-drawn neighborhood map are spread out. The mood is awe-inspiring, deeply emotional, and full of historical connection. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 10 — `page-10.webp`
> Miguel said, "This tree holds our whole neighborhood's history inside its roots."

Miguel stands up from his chair in the classroom, one arm raised and pointing toward the window where the great tree is visible outside, his expression earnest and passionate as he speaks. His red striped shirt is slightly rumpled from a long day of research. Valentina and the classmates sitting around the table look up at him with attentive, moved expressions, some nodding. The open historical books and photographs still cover the table. Warm golden late-afternoon light fills the classroom. The mood is impassioned, inspired, and communally galvanized. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 11 — `page-11.webp`
> Valentina wrote a strong letter asking the mayor to please save the old tree.

Valentina sits at a small wooden desk by a window in a cozy Dominican home with a coral-colored wall and a potted orchid on the sill, bent carefully over a large piece of white paper, her tongue slightly out in concentration as she writes a letter in careful large handwriting. Her thick black curly puffs frame her focused face. Beside her on the desk is a stack of research papers and the old photograph of the tree sapling. Through the window behind her, the canopy of the great mahogany tree is visible in the fading evening light. The mood is determined, focused, and quietly heroic. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 12 — `page-12.webp`
> The children walked door to door and collected more than two hundred signatures.

A bright sunny afternoon on Calle Verde showing Valentina in her yellow dress and Miguel in his red striped shirt walking side by side along the colorful neighborhood street, each holding a clipboard with papers attached. They stand at the bright pink door of a home where a smiling brown-skinned abuela in a floral dress eagerly signs her name on the petition sheet. Behind them, other classmates in school uniforms can be seen at neighboring doorways doing the same. Tropical plants line the street and laundry hangs between the colorful houses. The mood is hopeful, energetic, and warmly communal. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 13 — `page-13.webp`
> At the town meeting, Valentina stood up tall and spoke clearly for the tree that could not speak for itself, and the mayor smiled and said yes.

Inside a brightly lit Dominican town hall meeting room filled with rows of seated brown-skinned community members of all ages — abuelas, abuelos, parents, and children — Valentina stands alone at a wooden podium at the front, her posture tall and brave, chin lifted, speaking into a microphone with a clear and confident expression. She holds her handwritten letter in both hands. Miguel and her classmates sit in the front row watching her with proud, encouraging smiles. At a curved table to the side, the mayor — a stocky Dominican man in his 50s in a white guayabera — listens attentively and begins to smile warmly. Through the tall windows behind, the canopy of the great mahogany tree is visible under a glowing Caribbean sunset sky of orange and pink. The mood is triumphant, emotionally powerful, and full of hard-won hope. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.
