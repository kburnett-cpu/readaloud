# Illustration Prompt Guide: Mae's Big Dream

## Master Style

MASTER STYLE: Vibrant, warm children's book illustration in soft digital painting style. Clean outlines with rich saturated colors. Main character is Mae, a bright-eyed Dominican girl with warm brown skin, big dark curly hair often tied with colorful ribbons, and expressive dark brown eyes — shown at age 4-5 in early pages and growing to age 10-12 in later pages, usually wearing vivid Caribbean-colored dresses or school uniforms. Setting is the Dominican Republic: colorful painted homes in yellow, turquoise, and coral, lush tropical plants including hibiscus and palm trees, warm golden light, and close loving family including a round-faced mamá with natural hair and a silver-haired abuela in floral dresses. Illustrations feel joyful, warm, and emotionally rich, with expressive faces and lush tropical detail. 16:9 landscape format.

## Page Prompts

### Page 1 — `page-01.webp`
> When Mae was four years old, she looked up at the night sky and fell in love with the stars.

A tiny four-year-old Mae with warm brown skin, big curly pigtails tied with yellow ribbons, and wide wonder-filled dark eyes stands barefoot in a lush Dominican backyard at night, head tilted all the way back, mouth open in pure awe. The tropical night sky above her explodes with hundreds of glittering stars in deep indigo and violet, casting a soft silver glow across her small upturned face. Hibiscus plants and palm leaves frame the edges of the scene, and the warm golden light of the family's yellow house glows behind her. The mood is magical, tender, and full of possibility. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 2 — `page-02.webp`
> Every night, she pressed her nose to the window of her colorful yellow house and counted every glowing dot above.

Young Mae, age 4-5, with brown skin and curly dark hair in two puffs, kneels on a wooden chair and presses her small nose and both palms flat against a large open window of her family's bright yellow Caribbean home at night. Beyond the window, a rich indigo sky is scattered with glowing golden-white stars she is joyfully counting on her fingers. Warm lamplight from inside the room illuminates her face and the colorful floral curtains framing the window. The exterior of the house is painted cheerful yellow with teal shutters and a red bougainvillea climbing the wall. The scene feels cozy, dreamy, and full of childhood wonder. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 3 — `page-03.webp`
> Mae told her mamá, her abuela, and anyone who would listen that one day she would fly up to those stars.

Mae, age 5, with bright eyes and curly hair tied with a pink ribbon, stands in the middle of her lively Dominican family's living room with her arms stretched wide and her face full of joyful certainty, clearly mid-declaration. Her mamá — a warm brown-skinned woman with natural hair and a wide loving smile — kneels at her level, hands on her knees, laughing with delight. Abuela, a stout silver-haired woman in a bright floral dress, sits in a rattan chair beside them, clasping her hands together with proud, sparkling eyes. The room is rich with Caribbean color — tiled floors, potted plants, family photos on warm-painted walls. The mood is joyful, loving, and full of family warmth. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 4 — `page-04.webp`
> But at school, some classmates laughed when Mae said she wanted to be an astronaut.

Mae, now about age 7 with curly hair in a high ponytail and wearing a white school uniform blouse and navy skirt, stands at the front of a bright school classroom with her arms crossed tightly and her chin lifted bravely, though her eyes show a flicker of hurt. Around her, two or three classmates — a mix of children — are laughing and pointing, mouths wide open in exaggerated mockery. A chalkboard behind Mae reads the word 'ASTRONAUT' with a small drawing of a rocket. Warm Caribbean sunlight pours through tall classroom windows. The mood is courageous but emotionally stinging. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 5 — `page-05.webp`
> Her guidance counselor shook her head slowly and said, "Maybe you should think of a different dream, Mae."

Mae, age 8, in her school uniform, sits across a cluttered desk from a middle-aged guidance counselor — a woman with reading glasses pushed down her nose and a tight, doubtful expression — inside a small school office with file folders and a single wilting plant. The counselor's hand is raised in a slow, dismissive wave, and her head is tilted with a condescending half-shake. Mae's hands are folded in her lap and her expression is a careful mix of polite stillness and quiet resistance, her jaw set. A window behind the counselor shows a bright tropical sky outside, a visual reminder of Mae's dream world beyond. The mood is discouraging but hints at Mae's inner determination. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 6 — `page-06.webp`
> A quiet, mean little voice inside Mae began to whisper that maybe they were all right.

Mae, age 8, sits alone on the edge of her bed in her small colorful bedroom, hunched forward with her chin resting in both hands and her eyes cast downward in doubt. Around her head, illustrated as soft shadowy wisps of dark cloud, tiny discouraging words like 'impossible' and 'maybe they're right' swirl like whispers given visual form. On her bedroom wall behind her hangs a hand-drawn poster of the night sky and a cut-out photo of a rocket, slightly crumpled at the corner. The warm amber light of her bedside lamp creates a pool of soft light in an otherwise dimly lit room. The mood is melancholy and introspective, capturing the weight of self-doubt. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 7 — `page-07.webp`
> But then Mae thought of the stars, and she opened her books and studied harder than anyone else in her class.

Mae, age 8-9, sits upright at her wooden desk at home with determined bright eyes, her school books spread open wide in front of her, a pencil gripped tightly in her hand as she works with fierce focus. Through her bedroom window behind her, the star-filled Caribbean night sky is visible — and one bright star seems to glow especially warm and close, as if speaking to her. Her expression has shifted from sadness to fire, jaw set and eyes shining. Stacks of library books tower beside her on the desk. The room glows with warm golden lamplight. The mood is energized, resilient, and quietly triumphant. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 8 — `page-08.webp`
> She walked to the library every afternoon and taught herself calculus from thick books she could barely carry home.

Mae, age 9, with curly hair in a ponytail and wearing a bright teal dress, walks along a sun-dappled Caribbean street lined with colorful painted buildings and tropical trees toward a large public library building. She carries an enormous stack of thick math and science books clutched to her chest with both arms, the pile nearly as tall as her head and threatening to topple, but her face shows fierce and cheerful determination. One book spine clearly reads 'CALCULUS' in bold letters. The afternoon light is golden and warm. The mood is industrious, joyful, and quietly heroic. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 9 — `page-09.webp`
> Mae wrote careful letters to scientists at NASA, telling them about her love for space and her big, unbreakable dream.

Mae, age 9-10, sits at a sunlit wooden table near a tall library window, carefully writing a letter with her best handwriting on crisp white paper. Her tongue peeks out slightly in concentration, and her neat cursive fills the page. Beside her on the table sits an open book about NASA and a hand-drawn diagram of a rocket with labels in her own childlike writing. Through the library window, a brilliant blue Caribbean sky with a single wisp of cloud is visible. A small envelope addressed to 'NASA Scientists' sits ready beside her ink pen. The mood is hopeful, earnest, and quietly brave. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 10 — `page-10.webp`
> One bright morning, a letter arrived with the NASA seal on the front, and Mae's hands shook as she opened it.

Mae, age 10, stands at the front door of her family's bright yellow Dominican home on a sunny morning in her school uniform, holding a white envelope at arm's length with both hands trembling visibly. Her eyes are enormous — wide with disbelief and barely contained hope — and her mouth is parted in a gasp. The envelope clearly shows a bold blue circular NASA seal on the front. Behind her in the doorway, her mamá and abuela crowd together, leaning in with clasped hands and excited, breathless expressions. Warm morning tropical light floods the porch. The mood is electrifying and tender with anticipation. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 11 — `page-11.webp`
> The scientists had written back — they said her questions were brilliant and told her to keep reaching for the stars.

Mae, age 10, sits at her family's kitchen table with the NASA letter fully unfolded and spread before her, her face glowing with pure radiant joy — eyes shining with tears she is too happy to blink back, mouth stretched into the widest smile. Her mamá stands behind her with both hands pressed to her cheeks in joyful disbelief, and abuela's eyes are bright with tears of pride as she squeezes Mae's shoulder. The letter on the table has illustrated highlights — the words 'brilliant questions' and 'keep reaching for the stars' seem to glow softly on the page. Sunlight pours through the kitchen window over a vase of hibiscus flowers. The mood is euphoric, warm, and deeply affirming. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 12 — `page-12.webp`
> Mae kept studying, kept dreaming, and kept looking up, because she knew the sky was not the limit — it was just the beginning.

Mae, now age 11-12, older and taller with the same warm brown skin and thick curly hair — now wearing a confident expression and a bright yellow dress — stands in her tropical backyard at dusk surrounded by towering stacks of open science books, a star chart pinned to the fence beside her, and a handmade model rocket on the garden table. She looks upward toward a sky that transitions from warm orange sunset at the horizon to deep violet and the first emerging stars above. Her posture is strong, open, and full of quiet power. The whole scene radiates momentum — she is not finished, she is becoming. The mood is determined, luminous, and inspiring. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.

### Page 13 — `page-13.webp`
> One day, a girl just like Mae will look up at the night sky and know that she can go there too.

A new small girl — age 4-5, brown-skinned and curly-haired like young Mae, in a white nightgown — stands in a lush tropical backyard at night, gazing up at a vast star-filled sky in the same pose of wonder from page one. But now, among the stars, a tiny illustrated astronaut figure in a white spacesuit floats serenely — unmistakably Mae, older, with her curly hair visible through her helmet visor — looking back down at the little girl below with a warm smile. The stars around the astronaut shimmer extra brightly, as if celebrating. The mood is full circle, joyful, and profoundly hopeful, a promise passed from one dreamer to the next. Children's book illustration, 16:9 landscape.
